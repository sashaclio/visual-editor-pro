# Example Output

This is a sample output structure for Visual Editor Pro.

The example below is intentionally generic and is meant to demonstrate format, not to serve as a benchmark-quality diagnosis.

---

## 📷 Original Image Diagnosis

**Category**: Street / Documentary

**Issues found**:
- Highlights are slightly clipped on reflective surfaces, reducing texture retention.
- Shadow detail is compressed in the subject's clothing and background doorway.
- White balance leans slightly cool, making skin and concrete tones feel less natural.
- Midtone contrast is somewhat flat, so the image lacks depth separation.
- Global sharpness is acceptable, but the subject lacks local presence.

---

## 🎛 Retouching Parameters

### Lightroom

- Exposure → +0.20, to lift overall brightness without overextending highlights.
- Contrast → +12, to improve tonal separation.
- Highlights → -38, to recover bright areas on reflective surfaces.
- Shadows → +26, to restore detail in darker clothing and background areas.
- Whites → -10, to control upper-end brightness.
- Blacks → -14, to anchor the frame with cleaner black values.
- Texture → +12, to improve material detail.
- Clarity → +8, to strengthen midtone definition.
- Dehaze → +4, to improve micro-contrast subtly.
- Vibrance → +10, to restore muted color selectively.
- Saturation → -2, to keep the palette restrained.
- Temp → +4, to neutralize the cool cast.
- Tint → +3, to rebalance skin and concrete tones.
- HSL Orange Saturation → -6, to prevent skin-adjacent tones from becoming too warm.
- HSL Blue Luminance → -8, to deepen cool background tones.
- HSL Yellow Hue → -4, to stabilize mixed urban lighting.
- Tone Curve → Contrast-oriented, to strengthen depth while preserving realism.

- Sharpening Radius → 0.9, to keep edges natural.
- Sharpening Masking → 58, to avoid sharpening flat areas.
- Noise Reduction Luminance → 12, to smooth subtle digital noise.
- Noise Reduction Color → 18, to clean color noise in shadows.

## 🎨 Visual Direction

The generated direction should follow a restrained documentary-editorial language with soft urban contrast, controlled cool-to-neutral grading, and a clear sense of ambient realism. Lighting should remain believable and directional rather than cinematic for its own sake. Texture should feel photographic, with subtle film-like density instead of exaggerated clarity or glossy retouching.

---

## 🖼 Generated Result

A suitable downstream generation would aim for a documentary fashion image with realistic skin and fabric texture, medium-format tonal richness, and understated editorial styling rather than trend-driven filter effects.
